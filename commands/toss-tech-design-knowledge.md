## Thematic Summaries

### 1. Research as Operating Infrastructure (not just validation)
Toss treats UX research as org-wide infrastructure, not a bottleneck. Teams like the Research Platform Team, 리옵매 (Research Ops Manager), the 리서치 자율주행 project, 유저 무물 런치, and TNS all point to the same goal: enabling anyone on the team to design, conduct, and interpret research — not just UX researchers. The researcher's role shifts from "interviewer" to "problem definer and context translator."

### 2. Efficiency = Decomposing Small Actions (not launching big systems)
Toss solves efficiency problems in 4 steps: (1) break current workflows into atomic actions, (2) ask "why does this action need to exist?", (3) reprioritize by stakeholder impact — not personal convenience, (4) apply improvements in small testable increments, not all-at-once automation.

### 3. Product Design = Structural Redesign (not feature addition)
Most Product Design articles are about restructuring how problems are framed — removing unnecessary input, flipping the signup flow, recommending only one best option, reducing decision burden. Toss strongly prefers "fewer choices" over "more options."

### 4. Design System and Internal Tools = Treated as Independent Products
TDS color system update, menu component improvement, table component redesign, Framer workflow, data tool satisfaction, flowchart productivity — all are treated with the same product thinking as customer-facing products. Satisfaction scores, low-cost validation, reduced usage time — these are KPIs.

### 5. UX Writing = System Design (not copywriting talent)
Toss's UX Writing articles are about making the entire org write to the same standard — not writing for others. Templates, libraries, shared error message systems, marketing copy principles. Writing is treated as an interface design problem, not a personal craft.

### 6. Accessibility = Core Usability (not a compliance checklist)
Foreign user auth research, face recognition for visually impaired users, VoiceOver reading order, senior UX, children's products, braille card branding — all treated as first-class usability failures, not edge-case accommodations.

### 7. Brand, Graphic, Interaction = Functional Communication Tools
Brand symbol research, new face/persona design, Toss Product Sans, Tossface emoji font, AI graphic generator, homepage experience, interaction design — all framed as tools for cognitive speed, trust, immersion, and comprehension. Never decoration.

### 8. People and Culture = Designed like Products
Toss People series, internship retrospectives, design challenge design, first UX Writer / first UX Researcher / first Graphic Designer / first Interaction Designer stories, Simplicity conference series — all treat hiring, onboarding, and culture as design problems.

## Recurring Execution Principles

1. Never treat a large process as a unit — break it into atomic actions.
2. Ask "why does this action need to exist?" for each action. Separate what to remove vs. what to automate.
3. Prioritize by the resources and context of all stakeholders, not your own workflow.
4. Prefer small tests and incremental application over complete solutions.
5. Research means defining problems and translating context — not conducting interviews.
6. Design systems, internal tools, and writing guides are measured and improved like customer products.
7. Accessibility is a core usability problem that determines whether a feature is actually usable.
8. Visual elements are tools for cognitive speed, trust, immersion, and understanding — not decoration.
9. Toss learns new domains by going directly to the field and immersing in user context.
10. Ethics (trust over conversion rate) underlies many product design decisions.
